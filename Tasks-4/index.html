About Swayambhunath Temple

Swayambhunath, also known as the Monkey Temple, is one of the most revered religious sites in Nepal. Perched atop a hill in the Kathmandu Valley, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a major pilgrimage destination for both Buddhists and Hindus.

History & Significance

According to legend, the Kathmandu Valley was once a vast lake, and a divine lotus flower grew at its center. Bodhisattva Manjushri drained the lake, and the lotus settled on the hill where Swayambhunath now stands.

Major Attractions

  • The Stupa - A magnificent white dome with Buddha’s all-seeing eyes.
  • Prayer Wheels - Inscribed with sacred Buddhist mantras.
  • Monkey Habitat - Home to holy monkeys believed to be connected to Manjushri.
  • Panoramic Views - Stunning views of Kathmandu city from the hilltop.
